# Humanizer

Identifies and removes AI writing patterns to make text sound natural and human. Based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" catalog (WikiProject AI Cleanup).

## Components

**Skill: humanizer** — Activates automatically when you ask to humanize text, remove AI patterns, make something sound natural, or review drafted content for AI tells. Covers 23 named patterns across content, language, style, communication, and filler categories.

**Command: /humanize** — On-demand rewrite. Pass text directly or provide a file path.

## Usage

Ask naturally:
- "Humanize this blog post"
- "Make this email sound less like AI"
- "De-slop this"
- "Review this for AI patterns"

Or use the command:
- `/humanize Here is some text to rewrite...`

## What it catches

Content inflation (fake significance, vague attributions, promotional language), structural tells (rule-of-three, negative parallelisms, em dash overuse, synonym cycling), AI vocabulary clusters (delve, foster, landscape, tapestry, pivotal, etc.), communication artifacts (chatbot residue, sycophantic tone, knowledge-cutoff disclaimers), and filler/hedging.

## What it adds

Voice. Varied sentence rhythm, specific details over vague claims, first-person perspective where appropriate, opinions, and acknowledgment of complexity.
