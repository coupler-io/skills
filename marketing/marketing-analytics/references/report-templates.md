# Report Templates

Structured templates for marketing performance reports at different cadences. Adapt these to match the data available in the user's Coupler.io dataflows.

## Weekly Performance Snapshot

Quick-scan format designed for team standups and async updates. Aim for 1 page / 1 screen.

### Structure

**1. Headline** (1 sentence)
The single most important thing that happened this week.
> Example: "Paid search conversions hit a 6-week high after the landing page refresh."

**2. Top Metrics** (3-5 metrics)
Show each metric with its current value, week-over-week change, and status indicator.

| Metric | This Week | vs. Last Week | Status |
|--------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| [Primary KPI] | [value] | [+/-% change] | On track / Needs attention |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |

Choose metrics that map to current business priorities, not a fixed list.

**3. What Worked** (1-2 bullet points)
Specific wins backed by data. Name the channel, campaign, or tactic.

**4. What Needs Attention** (1-2 bullet points)
Underperformance or anomalies worth watching. Include the data point and a hypothesis.

**5. This Week's Priorities** (3-5 action items)
Concrete next steps that follow from the data. Each should be assignable to a person or team.

### When to use

- Weekly team meetings
- Async Slack/email updates
- Quick check-ins with leadership

## Monthly Marketing Report

Standard stakeholder report with enough depth for decision-making. Typically 3-5 pages.

### Structure

**1. Executive Summary** (3-5 sentences)
Answer: How did marketing perform this month against our goals? What's the headline?

**2. Key Metrics Dashboard**
Table showing each primary metric with:

- This month's value
- Last month's value (MoM change)
- Same month last year (YoY change, if available)
- Target (if set)
- Status (on/off track)

**3. Channel Performance**
For each active channel (email, paid, organic, social, content):

- 2-3 key metrics with MoM change
- Notable campaigns or initiatives
- What's working and what isn't

**4. Campaign Highlights**
Spotlight on 2-3 significant campaigns that launched or concluded:

- Objective and target audience
- Key results (reach, engagement, conversions, revenue)
- Learnings for future campaigns

**5. What Worked and What Didn't**

- Successes: specific tactics or experiments that outperformed, with data
- Underperformance: what missed expectations, with hypotheses on why

**6. Recommendations**
2-3 data-backed recommendations for next month. Each should include:

- The insight driving the recommendation
- The specific action
- The expected impact

**7. Budget: Spend vs. Plan**

- Total spend vs. budget
- Spend by channel vs. plan
- Any reallocation recommendations

### Visualization recommendations

- Line chart: primary KPI trend over the last 6 months
- Bar chart: channel comparison on key efficiency metric
- Table: detailed metrics by channel

## Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

Strategic review for leadership. Focuses on outcomes, trends, and forward-looking strategy. Typically 5-8 pages.

### Structure

**1. Quarter Performance vs. Goals**
For each quarterly goal, show:

- Target vs. actual
- % achievement
- Explanation for significant misses or overperformance

**2. Year-to-Date Trajectory**

- Are we on pace for annual goals?
- What needs to change to stay on track?
- Key trend lines with commentary

**3. Channel ROI Analysis**
For each channel:

- Total investment
- Total returns (conversions, revenue, pipeline)
- ROI or ROAS
- Trend vs. prior quarter
- Recommendation: increase, maintain, decrease, or restructure

**4. Campaign Performance Summary**
Aggregate view of all campaigns run during the quarter:

- Number of campaigns by type
- Average performance by campaign type
- Top 3 and bottom 3 campaigns (by primary metric)
- Common patterns in what worked

**5. Competitive and Market Observations**

- Any notable competitor moves observed in the data (share of voice shifts, bid price changes)
- Market trends affecting performance (seasonality, industry shifts)

**6. Strategic Recommendations**
3-5 recommendations for next quarter:

- Each grounded in data from this quarter
- Include expected impact and required resources
- Prioritized by impact/effort

**7. Budget Request and Allocation**

- Proposed budget for next quarter
- Allocation by channel with rationale
- Any new investments or experiments

**8. Key Experiments and Learnings**

- Tests run this quarter and their results
- Learnings that should inform future strategy
- Experiments planned for next quarter

### Visualization recommendations

- Line charts: quarterly trend of top 3 KPIs
- Stacked bar: spend allocation across channels by quarter
- Table: channel ROI comparison
- Pie/donut: budget allocation proposal (≤5 categories)

## Template Usage Guidelines

- These templates are starting points — adapt to what data is actually available in the user's Coupler.io dataflows
- If a section requires data the user doesn't have, skip it rather than guessing
- Always note data freshness: when was the dataflow last successfully run?
- If comparing periods, make sure both periods have complete data (don't compare a full week to a partial week)
- Round numbers appropriately: percentages to 1 decimal, currency to whole numbers for large values
- Use consistent formatting throughout: same date format, same number formatting, same status indicators
